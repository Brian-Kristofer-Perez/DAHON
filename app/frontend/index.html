Our Mission

DAHON aims to democratize access to plant disease identification technology, making it accessible to everyone from home gardeners to commercial farmers. By providing accurate diagnoses and treatment recommendations, we strive to reduce crop losses, minimize unnecessary pesticide use, and promote sustainable agricultural practices.

Our Technology

DAHON utilizes a fine-tuned RESNET-9 convolutional neural network, trained on thousands of plant disease images. Our model can identify diseases with high accuracy across 14 plant species, providing instant analysis and personalized treatment recommendations to help maintain plant health and improve yields.

Frequently Asked Questions

What will be the benefits of DAHON?

Through precise identification of plant diseases, DAHON assists gardeners, farmers, and students in learning more about the plants they care for. The app provides accurate diagnoses, treatment recommendations, and preventive measures, helping users maintain healthier plants and improve crop yields while reducing unnecessary pesticide use.

How did you train your model, and why did you choose this model?

Due to time constraints and limited compute resources, our team leveraged and fine-tuned an existing pre-trained RESNET-9 model. This approach allowed us to achieve high accuracy without requiring extensive computational resources. Additionally, we conducted thorough research on plants and diseases to provide accurate information and actionable recommendations for users.

Why does DAHON only cover 14 plant species?

Due to the nature of machine learning and limitations in compute resources and comprehensive datasets, we focused on 14 common plant species for our initial release. DAHON should be viewed as a functional prototype and proof of concept that future research can build upon. We plan to expand our coverage to include more plant species, disease classifications, and comprehensive information in future updates.

What makes DAHON different from similar plant disease identification tools?

While similar computer vision solutions exist, DAHON differentiates itself by using the RESNET-9 model architecture specifically optimized for plant disease identification. Our approach combines technical innovation with extensive agricultural research to provide not just identification but also detailed treatment recommendations and preventive measures. We've also designed DAHON with a focus on accessibility and ease of use for both home gardeners and commercial farmers.

How accurate is DAHON's disease identification?

DAHON achieves an average accuracy rate of over 90% for the 14 plant species it currently covers. The accuracy varies slightly depending on the quality of the image provided, lighting conditions, and the stage of the disease. For optimal results, we recommend taking clear, well-lit photos that focus specifically on the affected plant parts. We continuously refine our model to improve accuracy with each update.
